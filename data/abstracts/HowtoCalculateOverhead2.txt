
Understand that overhead costs are expenses that do not directly relate to your product.
Know that direct cost is the cost of creating a good or service.
Make a list of every expense for one month, quarter, or year.
Account for common overhead (indirect) costs.
Use old costs or estimates if you don't know your exact expenses yet.
Divide your list into direct and indirect costs based on your business model.
Add all of the indirect costs together to get your total overhead costs.
