
Take pain relievers.Make sure to take only those pain relievers prescribed or recommended by your doctor, and exactly as instructed.
Practice physical therapy exercises.While you are still in the hospital, you will work with physical therapists to learn how to recover your mobility.
Meet with a specialist about a prosthesis.A specialist will meet with you shortly after your operation to discuss fitting and using a prosthetic foot.
Learn to deal with phantom pain.Sensations or feelings of pain coming from a limb that is no longer there are known as phantom pain.
See a therapist or counselor.Losing part of your body can be emotionally stressful.
Seek out a support group.
Try relaxation techniques.
Try some breathing exercises.
Focus on positive things.
Learn everything you can about living as an amputee.
