
Determine if you have been denied coverage by reading your insurance policy carefully.
Read carefully through any pre-certification letter or Evidence of Benefits (EOB) statement that explains why your claim has been denied.
Prepare your appeal by gathering the appropriate documentation.
Follow-up with the HMO and inform your doctor after you have filed the appeal.
Expect a decision on your HMO appeal within the time-frame outlined in your insurance policy.
File a formal complaint with your state's department of insurance.
Consult an attorney skilled in health care litigation to determine if you should file a lawsuit against your insurance company if you've exhausted your rights to appeal.
