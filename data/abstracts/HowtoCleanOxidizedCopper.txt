
Verify the metal is copper.
Check for the presence of lacquer.
Wash the copper in warm, soapy water.
Remove lacquer by boiling copper in baking soda if desired.
Create a cleaning paste with vinegar, salt, and flour.
Apply the cleaning paste to the copper.
Polish away the paste with a microfiber cloth.When polishing the copper, try to follow the grain (direction) of the metal.
Rinse and dry the copper.
Coat copper with virgin olive oil.
Protect copper with lemon juice and salt.
Coat plain copper in a suitable lacquer.
