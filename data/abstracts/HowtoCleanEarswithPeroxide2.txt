
Position yourself.
Soften your earwax with oil.
Add the warm peroxide.
Flush the wax out with warm water.
Drain your ear completely.
Dry your ear gently.
Clean your other ear.
Perform this procedure as often as you need to.
Use peroxide weekly if you get “swimmer’s ear.” Swimmer’s ear, or otitis externa, is an infection of the outer ear (outside the ear drum) that many people get from swimming.
