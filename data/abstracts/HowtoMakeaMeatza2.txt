
Preheat your oven to 400°F (204°C).
Mix together the ground beef, dried herb, basil, garlic clove, salt, and pepper in a large mixing bowl.
Divide the mixture in half, then press each half into an 8-inch (20.32-centimeter) pie pan.
Bake the meat for 10 to 15 minutes, or until it is done.If you are baking a thicker crust, the meatza may take up to 20 to 25 minutes to cook.
Take the pans out of the oven.
Prepare your pizza seasoning if you wish to use a homemade sauce.
Start heating the oil, garlic, and seasoning for your pizza sauce.
Add the tomato paste and water, then simmer the sauce for 5 minutes.
Spread the sauce onto your meatzas.
Consider adding some cheese.
Add the rest of your toppings.
Bake the pizza for 10 to 15 more minutes, or until the top of the meatza is browned to your liking.The meat should be completely cooked through by this point.
Cut each pizza into fourths, then serve it.
