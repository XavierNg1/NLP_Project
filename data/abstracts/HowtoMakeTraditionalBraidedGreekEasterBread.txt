
In a large bowl, dissolve yeast in milk.
Stir in 1 cup of flour and 1/2 a cup of sugar.

,
Cover bowl with plastic wrap, or place it in a large clean unscented bag, and set aside for one hour.
Steep mahlepi in 1/2 a cup of simmering water.
Grind mastic gum with 1 teaspoon of sugar in a mortar, until the mastic gum is as fine as the grains of sugar.
Add mahlepi infusion, butter and five eggs to the yeast mixture, and combine thoroughly.
Stir with a wooden spoon until the dough is formed.
Form the dough into a ball shape, and transfer it to a lightly buttered bowl.

,
Cover it with either plastic wrap, or an unscented clean plastic bag, and let rise for two hours.
Punch the dough down, and transfer to a lightly floured surface.
Roll each piece of dough into a rope, they should be about 15 inches long.
Place each loaf on a buttered baking sheet, cover loosely with plastic wrap or an unscented clean plastic bag, and set aside to rise for an hour.
Pre-heat the oven to 350 degrees.
Transfer the loaves to the oven and bake until they are golden brown.
Finished.
