
Start the interview with light conversation.
Switch to the subject of the questioning abruptly.
Let the interrogated person tell you their story without interrupting them.
Have another person enter the room shortly after the interviewed person has finished their story.
Return in about 20 minutes.
Take about 5 minutes during which you may rearrange things on your desk, or scribble something on a scrap of paper.
Ask for details.
Combine the real questioning with irrelevant questions, leading the suspect into believing you have something on your mind.
