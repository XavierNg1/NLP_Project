
Request a name change packet from the South Carolina Law Enforcement Division (SLED).
Take the fingerprint card to your nearest police or sheriff’s office to get fingerprints.
Complete and send your screening request to the Department of Social Services.
Complete your criminal records check form and send it to SLED.
Draft your name change petition.
Gather the other documents that must be included with your petition.
File your petition at the clerk’s office of the family court with jurisdiction over the county where you live.
Attend your hearing.
Take your name change order to your nearest Social Security office to get your name changed on your Social Security card.
File a name change request with the DMV to have your name changed on your driver’s license or state ID.
Use your new Social Security card and driver’s license to have your name changed on other accounts and documents.
