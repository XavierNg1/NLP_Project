
Stay positive even when circumstances feel difficult.
Contact your lawyer before you go in if possible.
Obey the correction officers within the scope of the law.
Use the phone as soon as possible.
Ask for a single cell.
Never show fear to the other “guests.” For many people who frequent jail, fear is equated with weakness.
Avoid sleeping in a shared cell.
Never remove clothes or shoes, unless you wish to risk them being taken away by the other inmates.
Keep to yourself, if you can.
Refuse any favors, politely.
Be careful about looking at or engaging other inmates.
Own your crime.
