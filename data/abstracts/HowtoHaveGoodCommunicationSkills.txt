
Start a conversation with someone.
Master the art of small talk.
Consider what topics are appropriate for the situation.
Know how to transition small talk into a deeper conversation.
Talk about things that interest the other party.
Practice active listening.
End the conversation in a graceful manner.
Be aware of your body language.
Use a tone of voice appropriate for the context.
Consider individual differences and preferences.
Read non-verbal communication.
Have knowledge of your core needs and values.
Gain the courage to say “no.” Being able to tell someone “no” is a very powerful factor in assertiveness, but also in confidence-building.
State your opinions without attacking those of others.
Accept compliments and criticism graciously.
Use compromise sensibly.
