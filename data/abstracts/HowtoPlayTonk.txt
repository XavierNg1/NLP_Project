
Gather enough people to play.
Make sure your deck is full.
Discard the jokers.
Settle on the stakes if gambling.
Cut cards to settle on who deals first.
Deal the first hand.
Count the value of your cards.
Declare Tonk if you have one.
Start a stock- and discard pile if there aren’t any Tonks.
Play clockwise from the dealer.
Create “spreads.” As you draw and discard cards, keep those that will form either a “book” or “run.” To make a “book,” collect three or more of the same card from each suit.
Build on existing spreads.
Empty your hand first.
“Drop” to try winning before you empty your hand.
Use card values to determine the winner if the stockpile runs out.
