
Make a list of all criminal cases in which you’ve been a defendant.
Order your report (also called a “rap sheet”) directly from the FBI or from an FBI Channeler.
Contact the state identification bureaus.
Complete the required form.
Get finger-printed.
Bring the completed form with you to the electronic fingerprinting site.
Inspect all the information in the report in detail.
Look for incomplete entries.
Make note of incorrect entries.
Pay attention to double listings.
Be aware of open warrants.
Check for cases that should be sealed—but aren’t.
Dispute your FBI record.
Rectify incomplete, incorrect, or double-entry mistakes.
Fix warrant errors.
Correct the “sealing” status.
Follow up on the progress of the report corrections.
