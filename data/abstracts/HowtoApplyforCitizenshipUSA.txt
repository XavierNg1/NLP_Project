
Be at least 18 years old.
Show that you've lived as a permanent resident in the US for five consecutive years.
Be physically present in the United States.
Have good moral character.
Be able to read, write and speak basic English.
Have a basic knowledge of US history and government.
Demonstrate an attachment to the Constitution.
Complete the citizenship application.
Have two photographs taken.
Mail your application to a USCIS Lockbox facility.
Get fingerprinted.
Complete the interview.
Wait for a decision.
Attend a naturalization ceremony.
