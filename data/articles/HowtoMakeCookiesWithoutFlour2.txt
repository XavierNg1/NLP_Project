 Have two small bowls on your work space next to the eggs. Your eggs should be at room temperature. Crack each egg and separate the yolk from the egg white. It's helpful to let the egg white fall into one bowl so you can place the egg yolk in the other bowl. Do this for every egg.








Since you'll only need the egg whites, you can cover and refrigerate the egg yolks for another recipe (like lemon curd, creme brulee or a custard).
 Add the egg whites to your bowl of dry ingredients. Use a firm whisk to stir them completely. The dry ingredients will dissolve in the egg whites to create a dark thick cookie dough. Stir in the chopped nuts and chocolate.








You can use a rubber spatula to stir in the chopped nuts and chocolate if the dough gets too thick. Check the bottom of the mixing bowl to ensure there isn't any flour or cocoa that hasn't been stirred in.
