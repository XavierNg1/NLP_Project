 You can get this form and its instructions from the USCIS website: https://www.uscis.gov/i-407. Alternately, you can schedule an appointment with your local embassy or consulate and fill out the form there., You can type your information directly into the form or print neatly using black ink. Provide all requested information and write “N/A” if anything doesn’t apply. The form will ask for the following information:Alien Registration Number (A-Number)
Your full legal name
Personal information, such as date of birth, country of birth, and country of citizenship
The last date you left the U.S.
Your mailing address
The reasons for abandoning your green card
The documents you are surrendering in addition to your green card
Your signature

, Contact your local consulate to check how to submit. Generally, you can submit it in person or by mail. However, if you are somewhere without a local embassy or consulate, you can mail it to the nearest field office.Find the nearest field office at https://www.uscis.gov/about-us/find-uscis-office/international-immigration-offices.

If you mail in your form, remember to include your green card with it.
 The consulate might schedule an interview. The purpose is informational: they want to explain the consequences of giving up your green card so you can make an informed decision.
