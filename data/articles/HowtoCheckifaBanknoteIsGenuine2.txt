 Usually the obverse (front) has more raised inks than the reverse. The usual spot for raised inks are the effigy (the portrait of a notable person, an animal, etc.), the denomination (the number on the banknote), and the name of the central bank. Some banknotes also have signatures, usually of the governors or presidents of the central bank, which can also be printed in raised inks.


In polymer banknote where raised inks are uncommon, usually there are some points of the banknotes which are raised.
, Genuine notes should often not be entirely smooth. Instead, they should be rough and uneven.
