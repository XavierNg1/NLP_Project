While chokos can range from a darker green to white, you want to be sure to select one that is not multi-colored.The multi-coloring is a sign of aging, and a white typically means it’s overly ripe.In some countries, the large, white chokos are simply used to replant or to feed animals.
 The fruit should be small, roughly 6cm long, and without any bruising or indentation. This size indicates a younger fruit. The larger chokos are less flavorful, and somewhat unappetizing.
