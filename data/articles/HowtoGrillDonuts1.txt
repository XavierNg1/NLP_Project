 Grilling donuts doesn’t mean you have to make them from scratch. In fact, store-bought donuts are more likely to deliver the grilled dessert you’re craving. Go for traditional glazed donuts. You can even use day-old donuts if you have them sitting around your kitchen., Grilled donuts are easier and tastier if you make them with store-bought glazed donuts. You can, however, make your own donuts to grill. Making your own donuts will take between 2 and 3 hours. You will make dough, let it rise, cut donuts, fry donuts, and, finally, glaze the donuts., Once you have bought or made your own donuts, cut them in half. With a serrated knife, slice each donut vertically. You should end up with two donut halves that resemble the halves of a hamburger bun., Turn your gas grill on. In order to grill your donuts to perfection, you will need to make sure the grill is at the correct temperature. Turn your grill on to medium-high heat and make sure that it has reached the proper temperature before grilling your donuts., The last thing you want is for your delectable grilled donuts to stick to your grill! To make sure that you can easily remove your donuts after grilling, spray the grill surface with nonstick cooking spray. Olive oil or canola nonstick cooking spray work best., Using metal thongs, place the donuts on the hot grill. Grill the donuts for 20-30 seconds, flip the donuts, and grill for another 20-30 seconds. Remove the donuts from the grill with metal tongs and place on a platter.
