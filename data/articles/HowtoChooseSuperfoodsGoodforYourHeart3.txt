 Whole grains can help lower your risk of heart disease. They contain antioxidants and fiber, which help promote heart health. Whole grains include oatmeal, brown rice, quinoa, and whole wheat.Try replacing refined grains like white bread, white rice, and processed breads, cereals, and baked goods with whole grain alternatives.
 Flaxseeds are a superfood that have a lot of benefits, including heart health. They contain omega-3 fatty acids and fiber. Flaxseeds can lower cholesterol, help reduce plaque buildup, and reduce your risk of stroke and heart attack.


You can add flaxseeds to just about anything you eat. Put them in smoothies, cereal, yogurt, salads, sauces, or casseroles.
 Nuts are a heart superfood. They contain heart healthy fiber, along with vitamin E, which can lower your cholesterol. Many nuts, such as walnuts, contain omega-3 fatty acids, which can boost heart health.Eat almonds, walnuts, pistachios, macadamia nuts, and Brazil nuts.
Make sure to get unsalted varieties.
You should eat nuts in moderation because of their high calorie count. Eating too many nuts may cause you to gain weight.
 Chia seeds are a superfood that help your heart. They may help reduce inflammation, lower cholesterol, and lower blood pressure. Chia seeds may also help reverse the hardening of the arteries. They also contain omega-3 fatty acids.Put chia seeds into anything, like smoothies, yogurt, cereal, oatmeal, casseroles, or soups.
You can make chia seed pudding by soaking chia seeds in almond milk overnight.
