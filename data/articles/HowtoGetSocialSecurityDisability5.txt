 The Canadian Pension Plan (CPP) provides disability benefits to adults who contributed to the CPP while they worked, as well as children who have one parent on CPP disability. Click here for more information. The Child Disability Benefit (CDB) is available to families caring for a disabled child under the age of 18; find out more here.
 Disabled adults under the age of 65 and disabled children in the UK have access to a Disability Living Allowance (DLA). Click here to start the process.
 Australian citizens over 16 years of age who are permanently blind or unable to work for 2 years or more can apply for a Disability Support Pension. Click here for more information. Someone caring for a disabled child under 16 years of age is eligible for a Carer Allowance; go here to find out more.
