 To have full custody and visitation rights, the father of the child must be the legally recognized to be the child's parent. If a man is married to a child's mother when the child is born, then he is presumed to be the child's father.The other ways to establish paternity are an Acknowledgment of Paternity or a court order.


Acknowledgment of Paternity forms are usually given to the mother in the hospital at the time the baby is born.
To get a court order establishing paternity, contact your county's prosecuting attorney's office. The office may be able to file an action to establish paternity on your behalf., This form is available at https://www.courts.wa.gov/forms/?fa=forms.contribute&formID=34. The form is used to ask the court for a parenting plan and child support obligation. You will need to provide one of the following to demonstrate that you are the child's legally recognized father:


An Acknowledgement of Paternity. (If both parents signed one, you by writing to the Center for Health Statistics, P.O. Box 9709, Olympia, WA 98507-9709.)
The date that your paternity was determined by a court order.
A copy of the child's birth certificate listing you as the father.

If you attach an Acknowledgment of Paternity or a birth certificate with your forms, include a "Sealed Acknowledgment/Denial of Paternity/Birth Certificate (Cover Sheet) form, available at https://www.courts.wa.gov/forms/?fa=forms.contribute&formID=34.

 Most courts in Washington have a courthouse facilitator program, which helps people with family law cases. The facilitator's office is usually located within the courthouse.Facilitators do not represent you or give legal advice, but they can usually review your forms and help you with any questions you have about the legal process.
 Make copies of your forms and take them to the court clerk. The clerk will stamp the original and the copies as "filed" and retain the original document. Filing your forms initiates a court case. The clerk will contact you at a later time to give you a hearing date.
 You will need to have copies of your forms delivered by the sheriff, a professional process server, or someone over 18 and not involved in the case.Have that person complete and sign a "Return of Service" form, which is available at https://www.courts.wa.gov/forms/?fa=forms.contribute&formID=29, and file the form with the court.
 At the hearing, the judge will ask you to answer any questions he or she has about the paperwork you have submitted. The other parent will have a chance to argue why he or she might object to you having custody. Try to anticipate these arguments and figure out beforehand how you want to respond.
