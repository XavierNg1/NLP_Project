 The best cleaners for anodized aluminum are in the pH range of six to eight. Seven is neutral (as opposed to acidic or alkaline), so you want cleaners that are close to neutral.Anything too acidic or too basic can damage the surface.Avoid cleaners with chlorine.
Dishwashing soap or Bar Keepers Friend are a good choices.
While you may reach for baking soda as an easy and natural cleaner, it's not a good choice for anodized aluminum, as it is on the basic side of the pH scale. It can end up damaging the metal, which you don't want. However, the opposite is also true: acidic cleaners aren't great either. Basically, don't use anything too harsh on anodized aluminum.;
