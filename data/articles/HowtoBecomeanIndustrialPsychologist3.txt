 Typically, you are required to take courses in industrial psychology, statistics, research methods, organizational development, personnel issues and leadership topics.


Complete the program within the stipulated time period. Full-time attendance usually allows you to finish an industrial psychology master's program within 2 years. However, many universities allow for part-time attendance and require completion within a 5-year period.
 Many universities require a written master's thesis. Generally, you select a faculty member as a sponsor and demonstrate your knowledge of organizational psychology by focusing on a specific topic or issue in the field.
 Some universities require completion of a comprehensive test that covers a variety of industrial psychology topics. Study guidelines usually are provided for comprehensive exams.
