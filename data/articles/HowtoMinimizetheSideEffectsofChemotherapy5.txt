 Sometimes chemotherapy patients have mood changes after receiving treatments. Changes in mood can include anxiety, fear, uncertainty, anger, and sadness. Think about how you feel each day, or keep a journal to record your moods so you can recognize changes., Speak to your doctor if you are concerned about your mood changes. They may be able to help you determine if the changes are due to stress or are directly related to your treatments and/or medications. They may be able to change your medications to ones with less side effects., A mental health therapist can help you learn strategies to cope with your mood changes. You may be asked to participate in cognitive behavior therapy or other therapeutic methods.
