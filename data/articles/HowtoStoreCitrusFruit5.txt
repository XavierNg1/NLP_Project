 Limes generally have a long shelf-life. Unlike oranges or lemons, fresh limes can last up to 2-3 weeks at room temperature.Storing limes at room temperature also allows them to yield more juice., Refrigerated limes can be placed into a loosely sealed ziploc bag and will keep for up to 4 weeks.Unlike orange or yellow citrus fruits, limes will begin to turn brown as they age. Limes that are mostly brown are considered moldy and should not be eaten., Wash, peel and cut fruit into sections, removing membranes and seeds. Freezing whole limes may result in a mushy texture when thawed.
