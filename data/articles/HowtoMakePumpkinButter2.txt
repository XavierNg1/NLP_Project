 Cut the sugar pumpkins in half with a sharp knife. Use a spoon to scoop out the seeds and guts. Brush the pumpkins with oil and place facedown on a parchment paper lined baking sheet. Roast for 45-50 minutes at 350F. They are done when the skin is slightly darker and wrinkled and you can easily poke it with a fork. Let them cool, then remove the skin.Make sure you get sugar pumpkins, which are smaller at about 2-3 pounds and sweeter, and not larger carving pumpkins.
 Place the pumpkin and apple juice in a blender to puree. Blend until the pumpkin flesh is very smooth.


You can use apple cider instead of juice if you prefer.
 After the pumpkin is completely pureed, add the brown sugar, maple syrup, and spices. Blend well.


You can also add whiskey if you want. Try adding nuts and apple slices to the butter. You can increase, decrease, or substitute other spices, such as ginger and allspice.
 Spoon the puree into a medium saucepan. Cover and prop the lid open with a wooden spoon. Boil over a medium-high heat., Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes.


Change the cook time depending on the thickness you want.
 After the mixture has reached your desired consistency, remove from the heat. Then, stir in the vanilla.
 Let the butter cool completely. After it is cool, stir in the lemon juice and sea salt. Store in an airtight container, like a jar or Tupperware.


You can store it in the refrigerator for two to three weeks. You can freeze the butter for up to a year. Do not can pumpkin.
