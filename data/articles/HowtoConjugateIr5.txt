 The present perfect is used to indicate an action that has occurred and has been completed prior to the current time or state, without excluding the possibility that such an action will occur again.The tense involves a conjugated form of the helping verb, "haber," and the singular past participle of the word "ir."
Example: "I have gone to the store," He ido a la tienda.

yo: he ido

tú: has ido

él/ella/usted: ha ido

nosotros/-as: hemos ido

vosotros/-as: habéis ido

ellos/ellas/ustedes: han ido

, The future perfect tense describes a situation that will have happened."
