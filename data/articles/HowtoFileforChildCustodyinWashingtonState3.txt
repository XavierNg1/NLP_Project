 Ask the court clerk if your local court has a courthouse facilitator program. Facilitators do not represent you or give legal advice, but they can review your forms and help you with any questions you have about the process.
 Make copies of your forms and take them to the court clerk. The clerk will stamp the original and the copies as "filed" and retain the original document. Filing your forms initiates a court case. The clerk will contact you later to give you a hearing date.
 You will need to have copies of your forms delivered by someone over 18 and not involved in the case. You can also pay a professional process server or the sheriff's office to serve the forms for you.Have that person complete and sign a "Return of Service" form, which is available at https://www.courts.wa.gov/forms/?fa=forms.contribute&formID=29, and file the form with the court.
 At the hearing, the judge will ask you to answer any questions he or she has about the documents you filed. The other parent will have a chance to present evidence to try to prove that you are not the child's father. Once your paternity is established, you can request custody and a parenting plan.
